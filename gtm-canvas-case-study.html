§ 01 / Summary

Launching the product that had to prove the reposition.

GTM Canvas was SalesIntel's agentic-automation flagship, the product that had to make the new positioning real: not a better database, a system that runs the GTM motion for you. The hard part was the audience. They buy contact records and they were fatigued by "AI agent" claims. So the launch led with the job the agent does, building and prioritizing target lists and sequencing outreach, and treated "agentic" as the how, not the headline. I owned the full launch kit and shipped one coherent story across every surface a buyer and a rep would touch.

§ 02 / The Problem

Launching an agent into a data buyer's world.

GTM Canvas is genuinely agentic. It does not hand you a list of contacts and wish you luck. It builds the target list, prioritizes the accounts worth working, and tees up the outreach, automating the GTM motion instead of just feeding it.

But the audience had a fixed mental model of what SalesIntel sells: records. Accurate ones, lots of them, at a fair price. An autonomous workflow does not slot neatly into "how many contacts do I get." Launched as a feature, it reads as a nice-to-have bolted onto a database.

And "AI agent" was the most over-claimed phrase in B2B software that year. Lead a launch with "our new agentic AI" and you invite a tire-kicking demo audience, not urgency. The product was impressive. The framing was a trap.

Nobody wakes up wanting an "agent." They wake up wanting the list built, the right accounts surfaced, and the busywork gone. Lead with that. // The launch framing problem
§ 03 / The Investigation

What job is this actually hired for?

I mapped GTM Canvas to the unglamorous work it removes. Not the model architecture, the Tuesday-afternoon reality: hours of manual list-building, segmenting accounts by hand, stitching together sequences, and the hand-offs that drop between tools. I pulled the recurring "this took my team a week" pains out of win/loss and sales calls.

"Show me it builds the list and tees up the plays. I don't care what you call the engine under it." // What buyers actually said

That settled the launch frame. We would sell the outcome and the hours returned, and let buyers watch the agent do the work. Agentic is how it works, not why anyone buys it. The word earns its place only after the job is believed.

§ 04 / The Launch

Build the launch around the job.

01 The message
▼ From technology pitch to the job done

Don't launch the engine. Launch the result.

The positioning, hero, and landing page led with the outcome: your GTM motion runs itself, your team works the accounts that matter. The "how it works" was a simple three-step, find, prioritize, sequence, not an architecture diagram. "Agentic automation" shows up once, after the value has already landed.

Before · Tech-led
"Meet our agentic AI"
Lead with the engine. Invite the skepticism.
→
After · Job-led
The busywork runs itself.
Lists built, accounts prioritized, outreach sequenced for you.

Same product, same capability. The only thing that changed is what the buyer hears first: the hours they get back, not the technology that gives them back.

02 The launch kit
▼ One story across every surface

A launch is only as strong as its weakest asset.

An agent product fails in the gap between marketing's promise and the rep's demo. So I built the full kit off one job-first narrative and shipped it together, so the page, the pitch, and the proof all said the same thing.

DELIVERABLE 01
Narrative & Landing Page
Positioning and a landing page that lead with the outcome and the hours returned, with a clean three-step of what the agent actually does.
DELIVERABLE 02
Deck & One-pager
A launch deck and sales one-pager so AEs could walk a skeptical buyer from "another AI tool" to "this builds my list" without improvising.
DELIVERABLE 03

§ 05 / The Impact

A launch buyers could actually picture.

An agent product lives or dies on whether people can see it working and locate it in their own workflow. The launch was built to make both effortless.

OUTCOME 01
Legible on First Contact
Reframing an "AI agent" as a GTM motion that runs itself let buyers place GTM Canvas in their workflow immediately, instead of filing it under "AI we will look at later."
OUTCOME 02
Sellable From Day One
Positioning, deck, one-pager, and demo script shipped together, so reps were never left to explain an abstract agent on their own. The pitch matched the page.
OUTCOME 03
Proof You Can Watch
The launch video let prospects see the agent build and prioritize a list, turning the hardest part of an agentic launch, belief, into a short watch.
§ 06 / Strategic Overview

The PMM Takeaway.

// Launching into skepticism

When you launch an "AI" product to an audience that has heard every AI claim, the technology is the worst thing to lead with. Lead with the job it does and the hours it gives back.

Make it watchable. The fastest way to defeat "agent" skepticism is to let a buyer see the agent do the unglamorous work they were dreading.

And launch one story, not five. When the landing page, the deck, the one-pager, and the rep's demo all say the same thing, a hard product becomes easy to buy.
