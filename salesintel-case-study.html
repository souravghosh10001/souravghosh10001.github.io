§ 02 / The Problem

The "ZoomInfo Alternative" Trap.

SalesIntel had a genuinely strong data product, and the marketing said so. The deep pages still do: a 95% accuracy guarantee, human-verified contacts, a mobile set reverified every 90 days, a 2,000-person research team. All of it true. All of it leading with data accuracy.

The problem is that accuracy, volume, and price are exactly the axes a bigger, better-funded incumbent will always win. When your headline is a comparison to the category leader, you have already told the buyer who the standard is, and that you are the cheaper substitute for it.

Meanwhile the category had moved. Buyers had stopped rewarding record counts. They were drowning in contacts and starved for answers to one question: which of these accounts are actually in-market right now, and what do I do about it?

When your homepage headline is a comparison to the category leader, you have already conceded the category to them. They are the standard. You are the alternative. // The framing problem
§ 03 / The Investigation

Listening for what buyers actually reward.

This was not a guess. I synthesized win/loss across Gong, Outreach, and Salesforce, and I ran a weekly competitive digest on five competitors, an AI workflow that scraped, structured, and surfaced how ZoomInfo, Lusha, Apollo, Clay, and others were moving on pricing and positioning. Two patterns showed up again and again:

"We don't have a data problem. We have a 'which accounts are actually worth working this quarter' problem. Records are cheap. Pipeline that converts is not." // The pattern across win/loss

Deals were not won or lost on record counts. They were won or lost on whether the platform pointed reps at the right accounts and got them into a conversation. The thing buyers paid for was the outcome. Data is the input. Pipeline is the outcome. The positioning was selling the input.

§ 04 / The Pivot

Re-anchoring the buying criteria.

01 Narrative rebuild
▼ From data accuracy to pipeline outcomes

Stop selling the input. Sell the outcome.

I rebuilt the landing-page narrative off the new positioning. The hero stopped leading with accurate B2B contact data and started leading with the outcome the buyer is actually buying: turn buying signals into pipeline that converts. AI agents, GTM Canvas and Signal360, became the mechanism that gets you there, not a feature list bolted onto a database.

§ 06 / Strategic Overview

The PMM Takeaway.

// Re-anchoring the criteria

Repositioning is not writing new copy. It is changing the criteria buyers use to judge an entire category, and then proving the new criteria convert.

The fastest way to lose a category is to define yourself as the cheaper version of the leader. The moment your headline is "the alternative to X," you have handed X the standard and asked the buyer to grade you against it.

The landing page is where a reposition earns its keep. If the new story converts there, it earns the right to spread into enablement, sales, and the rest of the funnel. Pick the axis you can win on, prove it on the page, then propagate it everywhere reps speak.
